# (KV Cache) Lossy Compression

In [arXiv:2604.07639](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07639), the authors prove that ideal Hilbert space dimensionality of generally interacting qubit processors still imparts an exponential _storage_ advantage (like in AI/ML) even if **BQP=BPP**; in turn, we offer our best attempt to compress maximally-entropic data with _"quantum-inspired"_ (lossy) techniques, achieving roughly 328:1 compression ratio, commonly with > 95% inner-product L2 norm fidelity. We do this by loading 23 bits of data per real/imaginary component of a 16-qubit state, "cleaving" entanglement between two (arbitrary) 8-qubit subsystems, and compressing with a (4-bit) variant of Google Quantum AI's ["TurboQuant"](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.19874v1) (then decompressing and comparing L2 norm inner product to establish fidelity). These figures represent performance for a reasonable set of defaults, but the parameters are tunable.
